# Cybersecurity Basics Through CTFs
## Introduction
In this repository, we will learn the fundamentals of cybersecurity through common CTF (Capture The Flag) topics. We will begin with the core foundations such as networking, Linux, shell usage, and scripting, then gradually progress toward more advanced areas including web exploitation, cryptography, forensics, reverse engineering, binary exploitation, and privilege escalation.
Throughout this guide, we will combine theory with practical exercises and real challenges to reinforce every concept.
The goal is to keep the material concise and focused on what is truly essential. Some subjects, such as networking, can fill entire 800+ page books. Instead of overwhelming beginners with excessive theory, we will focus on the core knowledge required to start making real progress as quickly as possible.
Additional resources will be shared along the way for anyone who wants to dive deeper into specific topics.
Most importantly: start solving challenges as early as possible.
Cybersecurity is a hands-on field. Real learning comes from practice, experimentation, failure, and persistence. Reading theory alone is far less effective, especially when you are just starting.
